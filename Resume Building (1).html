Practical Guide to Writing FAANG-Ready Software Engineer Resumes

Writing a FAANG-ready software engineer resume requires careful attention to detail and showcasing the skills and experience that these top tech companies are looking for. Here's a practical guide to help you create an impressive resume:

  1. Clear and Professional Formatting:

    • Use a clean and professional resume template.
    • Maintain consistency in fonts, headings, and bullet points.

  2. Contact Information:

    • Include your full name, phone number, email address, and LinkedIn profile.
    • Ensure your email address is professional.

  3. Summary or Objective:

    • Write a brief summary or objective statement that highlights your career goals and what you bring to the table.
    • Tailor this section for the specific role you're applying for.

  4. Skills Section:

    • Create a skills section that lists your technical skills, such as programming languages, tools, and frameworks.
    • Include both hard and soft skills relevant to the job.

  5. Professional Experience:

    • Highlight your work experience in reverse chronological order (most recent job first).
    • Use bullet points to describe your achievements and responsibilities in each role.
    • Quantify your accomplishments with specific metrics (e.g., "Increased website traffic by 30%").
    • Emphasize relevant projects and technologies you've worked with.
    • Mention any open-source contributions, side projects, or personal achievements.

  6. Education:

    • List your educational background, including degrees, institutions, graduation dates, and GPAs if they are strong.
    • Include any relevant coursework or special achievements during your education.

  7. Certifications:

    • Mention any relevant certifications, especially if they demonstrate your expertise in a particular technology or area.

  8. Internships or Research Experience:

    • Include relevant internships or research experience, especially if you lack extensive professional experience.
    • Highlight any technical contributions and what you learned during these experiences.

  9. Projects:

    • Dedicate a section to showcase personal or side projects.
    • Describe the projects, the problems you solved, and the technologies used.
    • Provide GitHub or portfolio links for recruiters to explore your work.

  10. Publications and Presentations:

    • If applicable, include any research papers, technical publications, or conference presentations.

  11. Awards and Honors:

    • Mention any awards, scholarships, or honors you've received.

  12. Keywords and Tailoring:

    • Tailor your resume to the specific role and company you're applying for.
    • Use keywords from the job description and match your skills and experiences to the job requirements.

  13. Professional Associations:

    • If you are a member of relevant professional organizations, mention them.

  14. References:

    • It's not necessary to include references on your resume, but you can mention that they are available upon request.

  15. Proofread and Edit:

    • Carefully proofread your resume for typos, grammar, and formatting issues.
    • Consider seeking feedback from mentors, peers, or professional resume services.

  16. Online Presence:

    • Ensure that your LinkedIn profile is up-to-date and complements your resume.
    • Remove any unprofessional content from your social media profiles.

  17. Resume Length:

    • Keep your resume concise, typically within one or two pages.
    • Prioritize the most relevant and impressive information.

  18. Quantify Achievements:

    • Whenever possible, use numbers to quantify your achievements. For example, "Led a team of 5 engineers" or "Reduced server response time by 25%."

  19. Use Action Verbs:

    • Start each bullet point with a strong action verb (e.g., "Developed," "Managed," "Implemented").

  20. Customize Your Cover Letter:

    • Write a custom cover letter for each application, demonstrating your genuine interest in the company and role.
Creating an ATS-Friendly Software Engineer Resume

Preparing an ATS-Friendly Resume

Choose an ATS-Friendly Resume Template:

  • Select a clean and straightforward resume template. Avoid using elaborate designs, graphics, or complex formatting.
  • Use standard fonts like Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman. The ATS may have difficulty parsing unusual fonts.

Content and Structure:

  • Start with a clear and concise summary or objective statement at the top of your resume. This should highlight your career goals and what you bring to the role.
  • Organize your resume into distinct sections: Contact Information, Summary/Objective, Skills, Professional Experience, Education, Certifications, Projects, and any other relevant sections.

Optimize Your Resume with Keywords:

  • Use relevant keywords and phrases throughout your resume. These should align with the job description and the specific skills and qualifications the company is looking for.
  • Include technical skills, programming languages, tools, and technologies you are proficient in.
  • Tailor your resume for each application by customizing the keywords based on the specific job requirements.

Prioritize Information:

  • Highlight the most relevant information near the top of your resume. This includes your skills, recent work experience, and achievements.
  • Use bullet points to list your responsibilities and accomplishments. Start each bullet point with a strong action verb.

Quantify Achievements:

  • Whenever possible, provide quantifiable data to illustrate your achievements. For example, "Implemented a system that reduced server response time by 20%."

Test Your Resume Using Free Tools:

  • Utilize online ATS resume checker tools to ensure your resume is ATS-friendly. These tools analyze your resume's compatibility with applicant tracking systems.
  • Examples of free ATS resume checker tools include Jobscan, Resunate, and RezScore. These tools provide feedback and suggestions for improving your resume's ATS compatibility.

Review and Proofread:

  • Carefully review your resume to check for any spelling or grammatical errors.
  • Seek feedback from peers, mentors, or professional resume services to ensure your resume is clear, concise, and effectively communicates your qualifications.

Customize for Each Application:

  • Customize your resume for each job application. Tailor it to match the specific job requirements and company values.

Do's:

  1. Use Microsoft Word or Google Docs:

    Create and edit your resume using Microsoft Word or Google Docs. These platforms are ATS-friendly and offer standard formatting options.

  2. Submit as a PDF:

    Save and submit your final resume as a PDF file. PDFs preserve formatting, and they are easily readable by ATS tools. However, always create your resume in Microsoft Word or Google Docs first.

  3. Use Common Resume Formats:

    Stick to common and straightforward resume formats. ATS tools are designed to read and parse standard formats effectively.

  4. Narrow Margins:

    Reduce the margins of your resume to maximize space. A margin of 0.5 inches on each side is sufficient and helps fit more content on the page.

  5. Standard Fonts:

    Use standard and easily readable fonts such as Arial, Calibri, or Garamond. Avoid using new or decorative fonts that may not be recognized by ATS.

  6. Readable Font Size:

    Maintain a minimum font size of 10px for readability. This ensures that your resume is legible for both ATS tools and human readers.

  7. Standard Sections and Headings:

    Structure your resume with standard sections and headings. Common sections include Contact Information, Summary/Objective, Skills, Professional Experience, Education, Certifications, and Projects. Using recognized headings helps ATS tools identify and parse information accurately.

Don'ts:

  • Avoid Graphic Design Tools:

    Do not use graphic design software like Photoshop or online resume builders to create your resume. ATS tools may have difficulty reading content created in such tools.

  • Minimize Header and Footer Usage:

    Avoid using the header and footer sections in a Word or Google Docs file. Instead, reduce margins and include all necessary information in the body of the document. Headers and footers can be problematic for ATS tools.

  • Steer Clear of Uncommon Fonts:

    Do not use uncommon or decorative fonts that may convert letters into special characters, making the text unreadable to ATS tools.
Crafting an Effective Software Engineer Resume

Crafting an Effective Software Engineer Resume

Professional Summary:

  1. List Your Key Selling Points: Begin by listing your most important qualifications, experiences, and skills that align with the job descriptions you are targeting.
  2. Concise Summary: Summarize these selling points into a brief, well-crafted summary that is typically under 50 words. This should quickly convey why you are a strong fit for the job.
  3. Address the Why: Clearly state why you are an ideal candidate for the position. Highlight your unique skills or experiences that make you stand out.
  4. Use Active Language: Write your summary in an active voice using action words. This creates a sense of energy and engagement in your summary.
  5. Start with Your Job Role: Begin with a concise job title that describes your role, such as "Software Engineer" or "Front End Engineer."
  6. Create a Headline: Instead of labeling the section as "Professional Summary," craft a more concise and descriptive headline, typically under 10 words.

Here are some examples of effective headlines for your resume summary:

  • Software Engineer (Full Stack): Highlight your specialization and experience in web development with specific technologies.
  • Senior Front End Engineer: Emphasize your experience and passion for web technologies.
  • Software Engineering Lead: Showcase your leadership and experience in complex systems and team management.
  • Senior at University X: Highlight your student status, focus on AI and ML, and relevant internship experience.

Contact Information:

Ensure your contact information is accurate and professional:

  1. Must-Haves:
    • Your full name (at the top of the resume).
    • Personal phone number (avoid using your work phone).
    • Location (city, state, zip) to indicate whether you are a local or international candidate.
    • Professional email address (consider using Gmail if your current email is not professional).
    • LinkedIn profile URL.
  2. Good-to-Haves:
    • GitHub profile URL.
    • Personal website URL.
    • Stack Overflow profile URL.
    • Links to your profiles on platforms like Medium, competitive coding sites (CodeChef, HackerRank), or any relevant online presence.

Use "|" or tabs to separate this information as needed.

Skills:

In the skills section, highlight your technical capabilities effectively. Organize your skills like this:

Skill Summary: A brief overview or category of your skills.

List of Skills: Include your programming languages and tech stacks. You can indicate your familiarity or proficiency level, such as "Over 10,000 lines" for programming languages.

Here's an example structure for the skills section:

            Languages: Java | Python | C++
            Frameworks: React | Angular | Django
            Databases: MySQL | MongoDB | PostgreSQL

By following these guidelines, you can create a software engineer resume that effectively communicates your qualifications, experiences, and skills to potential employers. This can significantly improve your chances of getting noticed and securing job interviews with top tech companies.

Work Experience:

When listing your work experience, follow this format for each position:

        [Company or Organization], [Location] | [Job Title] | [Start and End Dates (MM/YYYY)]

For each job, provide a list of your top accomplishments, highlighting the scope of your role, required skills, and quantifiable outcomes. Use this format:

        [Accomplishment Summary] : [Action] that resulted in [Quantifiable Outcome]

Example:

        Facebook, Singapore | Front End Engineering Lead | 08/2018 - Present
        - Led a team of front-end engineers responsible for Meta's open-source projects, including React Native, Jest, Relay, and Reason.
        - Successfully maintained and improved Docusaurus v2, a static site generator used by over 7.6k projects on GitHub.

Education:

Your education should be included but typically comes after your work experience, especially if you have several years of experience. Follow this format:

        [Degree Name], [Year of Graduation (or expected graduation date)]
        [University Name], [Location]
        GPA: X.XX / 4.0 (Include GPA if it's above 3.50/4.00 or relevant to the job)
        List key achievements, leadership positions, relevant projects, societies, awards, etc.

Example:

        BSc in Computing, Computer Science, Graduation Year 2015
        National University of Singapore, Singapore
        GPA: 3.82 / 4.00 (Magna cum laude)
        - Dean's List
        - Valedictorian
        - President of hacker society

Projects:

Include at least two key projects you've contributed to. Provide your contributions and, if possible, link to the project on platforms like GitHub.

Example:

        facebook/docusaurus
        - Maintainer and lead engineer for Docusaurus v2, a static site generator powering documentation for Meta's open-source projects, including React Native, Jest, Relay, and Reason.
        - Used by over 7.6k projects on GitHub.

Awards, Accolades, and Certifications:

Only include achievements relevant to the job application. Use this format:

        [Year] [Quantification] [Competition]

Example:

        2016 | Best All-Round Product out of 50 teams | Facebook Hackathon

By structuring your resume in this way, you provide a clear, well-organized document that effectively communicates your qualifications, experience, and accomplishments to potential employers, increasing your chances of being shortlisted for interviews.

Must-haves to optimize your content:

Do's

  • Highlighting a few of your best achievements is better than including many "average" achievements in your resume
  • Use only 1 page for your resume

Don'ts

  • Do not list all your achievements just to showcase a greater quantity without filtering

Keyword optimization

Imagine you are a hiring manager or recruiter screening a resume while juggling many other tasks in your job - you simply won't have much time on each resume! When a hiring manager looks at a resume, they are in fact quickly scanning for keywords of skills or experiences that they value, before paying any additional attention to your resume.

Recruiters and ATS do that as well, but based on the job description that the hiring manager helped to write. That is why optimizing your resume based on job descriptions is very important.

INFO

Some ATS will determine the strength of your skills based on the frequency of a keyword in your resume, and others assign an estimated amount of experience for a skill based on its placement in your resume.

For instance, if your previous job experience was 3 years long and you mentioned handling Search engine marketing (SEM), the ATS will assume 5 years of SEM experience.

Include keywords from job descriptions into your resume

You should always analyze the job description for must-have and good-to-have skills or experiences and ensure the keywords are added to your resume.

Include them under the "Skills" section and pepper the same keywords into the "Work Experience" and "Education" sections. Be sure to closely imitate the language within the job description.

Remember to include the full version of common abbreviations as well. E.g. Amazon Web Services instead of AWS, Google Cloud Platform instead of GCP.

However, do not do keyword stuffing for the sake of it - always remember that the resume will be read by a recruiter or hiring manager eventually.

Optimize keyword frequency and placement

Analyze the job description and determine how important each skill and experience is, then optimize the frequency of the keyword according to its importance.
